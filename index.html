Recycling saves money... and the environment

Why is it a problem?

Globally over 380 million tons of plastic waste is produced annually and this number has been steadily increasing. Polyethylene terephthalate is one of those most commonly-used plastics that can be found mainly in plastic water bottles. However, its nonbiodegradable characteristics takes hundreds of years to naturally break down, and it has major ramifications on our natural ecosystems.

Mission statement

Plast.eco aims to promote the recycling of nonbiodegradable Polyethylene terephthalate and provide an accessible method for reusing efficiently to ensure a safe and healthy environment for communities of future generations.
